Jaden Springer



- In his freshman season, Springer averaged 21.8 points, 9.4 rebounds, and five assists per game, leading his team to its first state tournament appearance.
- As a sophomore, he continued to impress, averaging 24.7 points, 10.2 rebounds, 2.4 steals, and 2.4 blocks per game. Rocky River reached the state semifinals, and Springer was named Southwestern 4A Player of the Year.
- For his junior and senior seasons, Springer transferred to IMG Academy in Florida, joining a highly talented team. In his junior year, he averaged 15.6 points, 4.0 rebounds, and 4.0 assists per game, helping his team win the GEICO High School Nationals.
- During his senior season at IMG, Springer faced challenges due to an ankle injury but still managed to average 17.4 points, 5.5 rebounds, and 5.1 assists per game.
- Springer's exceptional performance earned him a selection to play in the McDonald's All-American Game, which unfortunately got canceled due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

He was then assigned to the Sixers' G League affiliate, the Delaware Blue Coats, on October 24, 2021. During his rookie year, Springer primarily played for the Blue Coats and only appeared in 2 regular season games for the 76ers.

In his sophomore season, Springer achieved success by winning the NBA G League title with the Blue Coats. Additionally, he was honored as the most valuable player of the championship game.


Keon Johnson



- Injuries and setbacks shaped the high school basketball journey of a player named Johnson.
- Two months before starting high school, he experienced a severe fireworks accident that resulted in open fractures in four fingers, causing him to undergo surgery and intensive physical therapy to save his hand from amputation. Despite this setback, Johnson played for The Webb School in Bell Buckle, Tennessee.
- In his sophomore season, Johnson displayed impressive skills, averaging 25.6 points, 10.2 rebounds, and 4.1 assists per game, which led to him receiving the Division II-A Tennessee Mr. Basketball award.
- As a junior, he continued to excel with averages of 25.3 points, 9.4 rebounds, and 3.7 assists per game, guiding his team to the Division II-A state semifinals. He was once again honored as Division II-A Tennessee Mr. Basketball.
Unfortunately, Johnson's senior season was cut short due to a meniscus injury.Despite playing in only four games, he showcased his abilities by averaging 30.5 points, 10.5 rebounds, and 5.8 assists per game before his season-ending injury.

Keon Johnson, drafted as the 21st pick in the 2021 NBA draft by the New York Knicks and traded to the Los Angeles Clippers, signed his rookie scale contract with the Clippers on August 6, 2021. However, he played in only 15 games for the Clippers during his rookie season.

On February 4, 2022, Johnson was traded to the Portland Trail Blazers along with Eric Bledsoe, Justise Winslow, and a future draft pick. He made his debut for the Trail Blazers on February 24 and had a career-high 20 points on April 1. Unfortunately, the Trail Blazers did not make the playoffs that season, finishing with a 27-55 record.

During the 2022 NBA Summer League, Johnson played for the Trail Blazers and helped the team win their second Las Vegas Summer League championship in franchise history. On March 27, 2023, he tied his career-high of 20 points in a game against the New Orleans Pelicans. However, two days later, it was announced that Johnson had suffered a broken finger during practice and would be out indefinitely.
